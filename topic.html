Meelad (Mawlid)

Understanding the Islamic perspective on celebrating the Prophet's birthday.

Introduction

Many Muslims around the world celebrate the Prophet’s birthday (Meelad or Mawlid). Scholars, however, differ on whether this practice has a basis in Islam or if it is a later innovation (Bid'ah).

Quranic Perspective

The Qur’an does not prescribe a specific day of celebration for the Prophet ﷺ. Instead, it emphasizes following his Sunnah and living by his guidance.

Hadith Evidence

There is no authentic Hadith where the Prophet ﷺ or his companions celebrated his birthday. Instead, the Prophet ﷺ fasted on Mondays and explained that it was the day he was born.

Scholarly Views
  • Some scholars allow Mawlid as long as it is celebrated with Quran recitation and remembrance of Allah.
  • Other scholars consider it a Bid'ah (innovation) since it was not practiced by the Prophet ﷺ or the early generations.
Conclusion

While Meelad is a widespread practice, Muslims should be cautious and prioritize authentic Sunnah practices. Love for the Prophet ﷺ is best expressed by following his teachings, not introducing new acts of worship.
